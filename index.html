About me



I am a research assistant at the chair for Public Economics at the University of Augsburg. My research currently focuses on approaches to explain human consumption decisions with adverse health effects and possible policy measures to counteract and avoid negative outcomes.

I compleded my Masters degree in Economics and Public Policy in 2014 at the University of Augsburg. My focus lay in economic modeling, time series analysis and econometrics. In my thesis I analyzed education decisions and earning profiles in a model of overlapping generations.

My hobbies include photography, cooking, programming and model painting and building.
